Which two websites are you most inspired by?

The two sites that inspire me are Leon's Poultry & Oysters and Paul Macgregor's site.

How do they exhibit effective use of space, size or balance?

Much like the website shown in video three for Frank Chimero, Leon's Poultry & Oysters is attempting to use a strong focal point to draw the users eyes to the left side of the screen and the restaurant title. The size of the font is also key in drawing the attention to the title, rather than the chicken graphic on the other side. Although I find the use of the main focal point to be relatively effective, I do think the chicken graphic is somewhat distracting and may need to be resized. "When everything is emphasized, nothing is emphasized".

Opposite of the design concepts utilized in the first site I chose Paul Macgregor's site does not have singular focal point. There are multiple items the eye is drawn to and the logo/title is de-emphasized to encourage interaction and site exploration from the user.

What makes them similar and different?

Besides the differences described above, the two site also differ in that Paul Macgregor's site has much more negative space, and uses images that are unevenly distributed. However, Leon's Poultry & Oysters uses very balanced elements. While I feel that Leon's site is more to the point, I find myself more interested in exploring Paul's and staying on the page for an extended period of time. Neither is bad, and I believe both achieve the desired goal for the end user.
